<Article title="The archaeological trail" lang="en" ssrLang={props.cookies.lang}>
    Cvinger has been attracting researchers since the 1870s. The first archaeological excavations, of the barrows and the settlement, took place in the last years of the 19 th century, followed in the 1930 by investigations in the western part of the hillfort. After World War II, the site was excavated between 1986 and 1991, when archaeologists dug six trenches aimed at establishing the chronological span of the hillfort, the technique used in rampart construction and the possible remains of buildings in the hillfort interior. The archaeological investigations also involved partially clearing later deposits in the Cvinger Cave, which was done in collaboration with cavers and locals, as well as partially reconstructing a section of the prehistoric rampart, setting up the first information panels and marking the circular trail. In 1990, setting up an archaeological trail was a pioneering work, as it is the earliest trail of its kind in Slovenia.

    The starting points for the trail are located in the forest close to the primary school (where the road forks towards Dolenje Polje), at the parking area in the vicinity of the modern-day cemetery and in the middle of Meniška vas. The trail leads along forest paths marked with the sign of a stylised situla and with information panels that offer an insight into the archaeological site. There are introductory panels located at each of the starting points, additional panels at the three entrances into the hillfort and specific panels at key points of the trail, presenting the partially reconstructed rampart, the Cvinger Cave, the embanked path, the ironworking-smelting area and the Branževec barrow cemetery. Direction indicators are set up at each junction to facilitate orientation in the forest. The 2.5 km long path to Cvinger (265 m above sea level) is not a demanding one, only involving the difference in altitude of 60 m, along which the visitor is invited to experience the surviving vestiges of the prehistory of Toplice. We advise the use of suitable footwear and … have a good walk!
